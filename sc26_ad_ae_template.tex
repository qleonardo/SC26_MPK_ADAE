\begin{description}
\item[$C_1$] We present FastMPK, a high-performance sparse matrix power kernel designed to run efficiently on both CPUs and GPUs.
%scalable blocking strategy to reuse matrix
%elements in cache by recursively partitioning UDG and executing Matrix-Power Kernel (MPK) on submatrices with a specific order.
%\item[$C_2$] We integrate symmetric SpMV with our cache reuse technique to develop a unified MPK implementation and further
%improve performance on symmetric sparse matrices.
\item[$C_2$] 
We propose a cache-reuse strategy and develop a dependency-aware parallelization scheme for FastMPK. %FastMPK can reduce the memory traffic of MPK. 
Our evaluations show significant advantages of FastMPK over state-of-the-art approaches. %implementations on both CPUs and GPUs.

\item[$C_3$] We demonstrate the practical impact of FastMPK by integrating it into a polynomial preconditioned GMRES solver, where it achieves end-to-end speedups with minimal preprocessing overhead.

\end{description}

\newartifact

\artrel

$A_3$ integrates a polynomial preconditioned GMRES solver in the Ginkgo library using FastMPK and the CSR-based MPK.

\artexp

The GMRES solver using FastMPK should be faster than the solver using CSR-based MPK on most of the matrices.

\arttime

The expected computational times of this artifact on the Intel Xeon Gold 6258R CPU and NVIDIA H100 GPU are about 20~mins and 10~mins, respectively.
